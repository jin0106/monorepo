Service Terms

Article 1 (Purpose)

●      This Agreement is intended to govern the rights, obligations, and responsibilities of the Parties necessary for the use of the order/payment/delivery agency and other related services provided by the Company to [Owner] through the "Neubie Order Platform."

Article 2 (Definitions)

●      In this Agreement, the following terms have the following meanings:

  1. "Customer" refers to a person who purchases [Owner]'s products through the Service.
  2. "Neubie Order Platform" refers to the web page and/or smartphone application developed and operated by the Company to provide the Service to [Owner].
  3. "Service" refers to services that may be determined according to agreements between the Company and [Owner] or policies of the Company.
  4. "Neubie Order Service" refers to intermediary services for orders from Customers to [Owner], payment, payment agency, delivery agency, and other related services through the Neubie Order Platform.
  5. "Platform Fee" refers to the usage fee for using the platform imposed on each order.
  6. "Delivery Distance" refers to the distance between [Owner]'s shop and the final delivery destination where Customers accept products, and the specific calculation follows the Company's policy.
  7. "External Payment Fee" refers to external payment agency fees incurred by Customers when using payment services.
  8. "Neubie Order Manager" refers to the owner-specific homepage provided by the Company to [Owner].
  9. "Essential Information" refers to information necessary for [Owner] to use the Service smoothly and legally, including business registration number, shop address, phone number, business category, menu, operating hours, origin information, information about allergenic substances, etc.
  10. "Abuse Act" refers to actions by [Owner] that seriously violate good faith, such as false orders and improper profits, etc. 

Article 3 (Publication and Amendment of the Agreement)

  1. The Company posts this Agreement on the Neubie Order Manager.
  2. The Company may amend this Agreement within the scope not violating relevant laws, such as the Cybercrime Prevention Act and the Personal Data Protection Act.
  3. In the event of amending this Agreement, the Company shall post the details of the amendment and the effective date of the amended Agreement on the [Owner] site seven days before the effective date of the amended Agreement. In cases where the changes are disadvantageous to [Owner] or are of material importance, the Company shall notify [Owner] through the [Owner] site 30 days prior to the effective date of the amended Agreement.
  4. [Owner] is responsible for regularly checking the amendments to this Agreement by visiting the [Owner] site. Even if the normal notice has been made, the Company shall not be liable for any loss incurred due to [Owner]'s failure to confirm the amendment.
  5. In the event that the "Owner" does not agree to the amendment of this article, they can terminate the application of the revised terms and conditions for the "Service" and this Agreement by expressing their intent not to agree to the Company until the day before the effective date of the amended terms and conditions. Failure to make such an expression of intent shall be considered as an agreement to the amendment of the terms and conditions.

  1.   3. If necessary under relevant laws, the Company may post the contents of business suspensions, fines, etc., imposed on [Owner] on the Neubie Order Platform.
  4. If Customer complaints arise during the delivery process, the Company must sincerely resolve them.
  5. In the event of disputes between [Owner] and Customers, the Company must make efforts for mediation.

  3. If there are changes to the "essential information," the "Owner" must promptly notify the "Company" and make the necessary updates.
  4. In the event of closure or any other reason affecting their business, the "Owner" must inform the "Company."
  5. The "Owner" is responsible for any damages or legal violations resulting from the failure to inform the "Company" about changes in "essential information."
  6. The "Owner" should not unreasonably reject orders from "Customers" who wish to purchase their products through the "Neubie Order Platform."
  7. The "Owner" is responsible for the quality of their products for "Customers." In case "Customers" or third parties file claims for compensation or other issues against the "Company," the "Owner" is obligated to indemnify the "Company" for all losses incurred.
  8. When using the "Neubie Order Service" to relay "Customer" information to a third party for delivery or other purposes, the "Owner" must transmit only the minimal order-related information required for the delivery. They must securely dispose of "Customer" personal information once the delivery is complete, failing which they bear responsibility.

  10. The "Owner" must not engage in verbal abuse, physical assault, defamation, sexual harassment, or similar conduct towards the "Company" employees, third parties commissioned by the "Company," and their employees.

  1. The "Owner" must package products appropriately for delivery based on their nature, weight, size, etc.
  2. If the packaging is not suitable for delivery, the "Company" may request the "Owner" to repackage the items or, with the "Owner's" consent, do the repackaging to ensure safe delivery. If this incurs additional repackaging costs, the "Company" can bill them to the "Owner."
  3. For the smooth operation of the "Service," the "Company" may confirm the types and quantities of products with the consent of the "Owner."
  4. In the event of significant delays, accidents during delivery, product damage, or the possibility of product deterioration from the moment the "Company" receives the products until the time of delivery to "Customers," the "Company" must promptly inform the "Owner."
  5. Liability for delivery accidents, product damage, and significant delivery delays begins once the "Owner" hands over the products. If disputes are not resolved amicably, they will be handled according to the Consumer Dispute Resolution Standards of the Fair Trade Commission.
  6. If "Customers" are unable to receive products due to their own fault for reasons listed below, they still have an obligation to pay, and refunds as per Article 9, Section 5 will not apply:
  1. When "Customers" wrongly register or write the delivery address due to their own negligence.
  2. When the "Customers" have been contacted multiple times but are unresponsive.
  3. When "Customers" have requested the delivery of products or services that violate the terms of service.

Article 9 (Payment Settlement and Refunds)

  1. The "Company" will pay the "Owner" according to its settlement cycle after deducting "External Payment Fees" and "Platform Fees."
  2. Settlement of "External Payment Fees" occurs on a weekly basis, with the following schedule:
  • The "Company" will pay the amount, minus the "Platform Fees" for the period from Thursday to Wednesday received from "Customers," within five business days of Wednesday, and in cases where the fifth business day falls on a holiday, the settlement will be made the next day.
  1. If the "Owner" wishes to change the method of receiving settlement payments, they must notify the "Company" seven business days in advance for verbal notifications and ten business days in advance for written notifications, counting from the settlement date. The "Company" shall not be responsible for any uncertain or unnotified changes resulting in losses.
  2. If the "Owner" intends to transfer settlement payments generated through the "Service" to a third party, they must obtain prior consent from the "Company." Transfers without such consent will not be effective against the "Company."
  3. In cases where a refund to "Customers" is necessary due to the fault of one party, either the "Company" or the "Owner," the party responsible bears the full cost of the refund. If it is challenging to determine which party is at fault, the "Company" will, in accordance with its internal refund policy, first refund the "Customer." Subsequently, the "Owner" and the "Company" will engage in discussions to determine the portion for which each party is at fault, and the "Owner" must make the agreed-upon refund payment to the "Company" within three business days from the date of the final agreement.
